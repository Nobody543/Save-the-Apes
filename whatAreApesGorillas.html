Gorillas are one of the five kinds of Great Ape. There are two species of gorilla, these are the Eastern and the Western Gorillas. Each of these species have subspecies.


The Mountain Gorilla is one of these subspecies, and these gorillas are some of the most endangered Great Apes in the world. Here is a fact file on them:


Scientific Name: Gorilla Beringei Beringei

Height: When they are standing on two feet, they are between 4 and 6 ft.

Weight: Between 200 and 485 lb.

Diet: Over 85% of their diet is made up of leaves, shoots, buds and stems.

Habitat: Hight Altitude Forests

Population: About 1000

Fun Fact: Did you know that a group of gorillas is called a troop.
