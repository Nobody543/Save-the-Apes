Orangutans are one of the five kinds of Great Ape. Bornean and Sumatran Orangutans are two of the orangutans that we know most about, however, they are both critically endangered.


Here is a fact file on Bornean Orangutans:


Scientific Name: Pongo Pygmaeus

Height: 3.3-4.6 ft.

Weight: Most are 66-200 lbs

Habitat: Tropical, lowland and swampy rainforests in Borneo

Population: In 2003, the highest estimate for the number of orangutans was 104, 700. This number has most likely declined by a significant amount since this time. One research study projected that by 2025, this would have declined to 47,000.

Fun Fact: Did you know that Orangutans usually only give birth once every 8 years.



Here is a fact file on Sumatran Orangutans:


Scientific Name: Pongo Abelii

Size: 3-5 ft.

Weight: 66-198 lbs

Habitat: Tropical and subtropical, moist broadleaf forests

Population: In 2004, it was estimated that there were around 7000 Sumatran orangutans left. It is now estimated that there are around 14, 500.

Fun Fact: Did you know that in Malaysian and Indonesian languages, ‘orang utan’ literally means ‘person of the forest’!
